Oblivion

Thu 05 September 2019   Al Faaz   Poetry Solitude

She steals up to my side quietly as I sit by the window, watching the evening shadows fade. Stray sounds of life outside come in through the glass, borne on the still, cold air of dusk, but fall, unheard, on my ears, and perish. The clock ticks in the background somewhere, futilely.

Unaware of her presence, unmolested in my reverie I sit, for unending time, until the shadows disappear, the sounds dim, and the evening sinks into the night. Her being is more palpable for the darkness, but I only notice her when she encloses me in an embrace that doesn’t let go. At the lightness of her touch, I look up, surprised out of my ruminations. But even her presence cannot stop the flow of thoughts, thoughts of different times, some lost in the past, some lost to the future. So in that darkness we sit, she and I, in companionable silence, sharing that indeterminate space between memory and fantasy.

When the only sound is the remorseless ticking of the clock, she beckons me to the nearly empty room that lies at the end of the short hallway a few miles away. The only things there are my bed and sundry belongings, which I must have left untended.

Obeying her unspoken invitation, I follow her to the room. There’s nothing to do there but lie down, and gaze, as I often do, through the lone window into the dark abyss of the sky. The prospect of bed is faintly pleasant, but sleep is elusive. I lay my head in her lap and wait.

Presently, she begins playing with my hair and caressing my brow, her touch soft, and as my eyes start to close, I feel a reluctant peace returning. Her spell is working, and soon I shall have forgotten everything. I wish again that I could stay here forever beside her gossamer form, but I know that won’t happen. She’ll be gone when I wake, leaving me behind to yearn another day. But she’ll come back, she always does, and we’ll do this little dance once more.

She’s my truest companion — the Night — and I shall wait for her to return tomorrow, when she’ll bring me her most precious gift again. Oblivion.
